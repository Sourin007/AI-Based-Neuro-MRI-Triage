# Source-Grounded Clinical Pathways

This corpus supplements the treatment pathway section with conservative, source-grounded summaries for clinician review. It is advisory only and should not replace specialty care, formal staging, pathology, or guideline-based treatment planning.

## Glioma Pathway

Source: National Cancer Institute, Adult Central Nervous System Tumors Treatment (PDQ) Patient Version. Link: https://www.cancer.gov/types/brain/patient/adult-brain-treatment-pdq

For suspected adult glioma, typical management begins with specialist neuroradiology and neurosurgical review, with surgery used when feasible to obtain tissue diagnosis and reduce tumor burden. Depending on tumor type and grade, additional management may include radiation therapy, chemotherapy, or combined chemoradiation after surgery. When imaging is concerning but uncertainty remains, contrast-enhanced MRI review, comparison with prior imaging, and multidisciplinary neuro-oncology discussion are appropriate before treatment decisions.

## Meningioma Pathway

Source: National Cancer Institute, Adult Central Nervous System Tumors Treatment (PDQ) Patient Version. Link: https://www.cancer.gov/types/brain/patient/adult-brain-treatment-pdq

Source: National Cancer Institute, Meningioma: Diagnosis and Treatment. Link: https://www.cancer.gov/rare-brain-spine-tumor/tumors/meningioma

For suspected meningioma, next steps commonly depend on symptoms, tumor size, and grade suspicion. Grade I meningiomas without symptoms may be observed with surveillance imaging. Symptomatic lesions or lesions causing mass effect are typically reviewed for surgery, and radiation may be considered if residual tumor remains or surgery is not feasible. Smaller tumors may be considered for stereotactic radiosurgery when clinically appropriate. Higher-grade or recurrent meningiomas are often discussed for surgery plus radiation, with clinical trials considered in selected cases.

## Pituitary Pathway

Source: National Cancer Institute, Pituitary Tumors Treatment (PDQ) Patient Version. Link: https://www.cancer.gov/types/pituitary/patient/pituitary-treatment-pdq

Source: MedlinePlus, Pituitary Tumors. Link: https://medlineplus.gov/pituitarytumors.html

For suspected pituitary tumors, review often includes dedicated sellar MRI interpretation, endocrine laboratory correlation, and visual assessment when there are visual symptoms or optic pathway concerns. Treatment commonly includes transsphenoidal surgery when feasible, especially for symptomatic or hormonally active tumors. Depending on tumor subtype and residual disease, radiation therapy, medical therapy, or structured follow-up may also be considered.

## No Tumor / Indeterminate Pathway

Source: National Cancer Institute, Adult Central Nervous System Tumors Treatment (PDQ) Patient Version. Link: https://www.cancer.gov/types/brain/patient/adult-brain-treatment-pdq

When the AI output does not support a clear tumor call, the pathway should remain cautious. If symptoms persist or image quality is limited, clinicians may still pursue full neuroradiology review, contrast-enhanced MRI, interval follow-up imaging, or additional workup based on the clinical picture. Active surveillance is generally reserved for selected slow-growing or asymptomatic scenarios after specialist assessment rather than as an automated AI conclusion.
